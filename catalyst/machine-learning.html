Definitions and Types

  • Supervised, network learns correlation between labels and data

The Mechanics of Training (Supervised)

  • Needs a large classified corpus
  • Generating a corpus is a time consuming manual process
  • Training takes a long time
  • Training is a lot quicker if you have powerful GPUs
  • Powerful GPUs are expensive

GPUs

  • GPUs are essential for many deep learning tasks
  • GPUs are currently not easy to virtualise (eg no support in OpenStack)
  • GPU vendors (Nvidia and ATI) are repositioning themselves as Deep Learning Companies
  • "NVIDIA GPUs - The Engine of Deep Learning"

Sourcing Data

  • Data is valuable
  • Where will you get data from?
  • What sources of do you have access to?

Opportunities

  • Identify data
  • Providing ML skills to clients to help them use their data
  • Partnering to get access to data
  • Evaluating data that may be valuable
  • Applying ML to running own systems better (eg Anomaly Detection in monitoring systems or data center power optimisation)

Risks

  • Machine Learning allows decoupling of results from human effort
  • If your competitors can use ML effectively they could accelerate rapidly
  • First mover feedback loops could allow first to utilise to capture significant market share
